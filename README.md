# Tree/House

It's a treehouse, but not.

A tree is not fully a house, unless you are a child, or adherent to an architectural neotony [sp?].

Retention of natal characteristics into adulthood. Before we evolved down out of trees, belled back from life's edenic recess. Far back in time circling sap. 

In the book the Baron in the Trees, by I. C., a little boy refuses to come down from the trees <--an arboreal retreat--> for dinner, and then also misses bedtime, as a way of avoiding punishment. They just stay in the tree forever, their whole lives.

Of course, in order for such a fantasy to be plausible there has to be a world-setting where trees are close enough together to tarzan between them.

It's the same thing with writing. Let's not pretend these little starts and branches are anything without the layering readings of seasons. Hermetic jars of stories, without yeast or mold of other/ancillary purpose. 


"If seeds are sown while the preceding crop is still ripening in the field, those seeds will germinate ahead of the weeds."

I didn't write down where that quote was from, nor do I remember. The seed of it germinated before the fatal flowering of attribution. 

According to Saussure, a linguistic sign is a combination of signifier and signified, the language or thought-image for the thing and --I'm simplifying here, mythologizing, abstracting-- the thing. The example they use is a tree. Arbor. <!--Its cells, its self-->

**Tree/Arbor**, I wrote in chalk on the tar paper by the stairs, like I was making a tree of files, of folding. *Building a story. Writing a house.*

J came by, wrote more, in places I'm not tall enough to reach.

Chase, I mean. Chase writes these things, not me.
